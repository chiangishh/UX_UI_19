Stop Asian American Pacific Islander Hate and Start Spreading Awareness!

Overview

Stop Asian Hate is a social movement mobile app that ncourage positivity and awareness for the Stop AAPI Hate social movement.

Role :

User Experience & Interface Design, User Research

Team :

Vivian Chiang, Michelle Lee, Amy Truong

Tools Used :

Figma, Miro, Photoshop, Trello

Duration :

2 weeks

- Background

What is AAPI?

A.sian A.merican P.acific I.slander

AAPI Hate

SAH reported 3,800 firsthand complaints of racisim & discrimination in the past year

Overall, hate crimes decreased by 7% while AAPI Hate Crimes increased by 150%

Over 200,000 Asian-owned small businesses in the U.S. closed between February to April 2020.

Why we chose AAPI Hate...

As an Asian American team, we wanted to spread awareness, create positivity, and alliances for our community.

Did you know that 233,000 Asian-owned small businesses in the U.S. closed between February to April 2020, including half of the country's Chinese restaurants. Research cited by Restaurant Business Magazine showed "consumer prejudice and misperceptions" as factors.

- Problem

How might we help AAPI supporters successfully report hate crimes with a fearless approach while spreading awareness and positivity in hopes to create alliances?

We believe that by designing an application focused on promoting optimism, community, and credibility for supporters of the AAPI movement, we’ll help reduce AAPI hate crimes and fear for victims.

This will be accomplished through allyships with movements like Black Lives Matter to form a sense of community. Additionally, we’ll promote events, provide visual updates of hate crimes via a map and live feed, and share credible resources that will spread awareness to protect the AAPI community. Finally, we will highlight the progress that has been made to increase positivity and reduce fear.

- Research

To understand user pain points with the social movement, we created a Google form survey and interviewed individuals through Zoom.

- Interview Insights

We learned that AAPI supporters feel strongly about recent hate crimes against the community and want to spread awareness and decrease crime.

We have found that young supporters feel enraged for the AAPI community, while the older demographics are experiencing fear. We as UX Designers would like to help by creating an application designed to promote optimism while spreading awareness of the grave situation.

- User Persona

- Wireframing & Low-fi

We began our design process by sketching and creating a low fidelity mockup. These were the initial wireframes used during user testing!

- Testing

As a group, we decided to focus on one out of the three main features, events. We tested the users' priority in the events feature. We asked the users to give us a walkthrough with the app and to think out loud. The two main questions were...

1) Do they care for event recaps?

2) Do they prefer dark or light UI and why?

- Iterations

We started off with a dark ui to match the website, however after user testing different color palettes, we decided with lighter UI. It aligned better with the positive and empowering theme. The new color palette was more empowering where it represented flesh tone. We also added brush strokes to multiple elements in our UI to create a sense of urgency to represent the importance of taking immediate action.
